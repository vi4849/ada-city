ADA Accessibility

What is ADA?

The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) is a landmark civil rights law enacted in 1990 that prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities. It ensures equal opportunities in various areas of public life, including employment, transportation, and access to goods and services.

ADA Standards for Accessible Design

The ADA Standards for Accessible Design provide guidelines for making buildings and facilities physically accessible to people with disabilities:

  • Newly constructed buildings and facilities
  • Alterations to existing buildings
  • Architectural changes in existing state and local government buildings
  • Removal of architectural barriers in existing businesses

What Makes Something ADA Accessible?

ADA accessibility ensures that individuals with disabilities have free and full access to the same programs, services, and areas as those without disabilities. Key elements include:

  • Accessible Routes: At least 36" wide, with specific slope requirements.
  • Curb Ramps: At least 36" wide with specific slope requirements.
  • Entrances: Door openings at least 32" wide with easily operable hardware.
  • Parking: Specific number of accessible spaces based on total parking spaces.
  • Website Accessibility: Ensuring websites are perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust for all users.
